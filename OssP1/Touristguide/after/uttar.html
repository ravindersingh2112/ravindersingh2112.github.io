Uttar Pradesh

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a world heritage site and best example of Mughal architecture in India. Taj Mahal attracts a large number of tourists and most visited historical monuments in Uttar Pradesh, located on the south bank of the Yamuna river.

Agra Fort

Agra Fort is the masterpiece of the Mughal Dynasty and a world heritage site, located about 2.5 km northwest the Taj Mahal. The historical fort has Jahangiri Mahal,Akbari Mahal and four grandest gates built of red sandstone.

Fatehpur Sikri

Fatehpur Sikri is an abandoned city and best preserved examples of Mughal architecture in India. The most important buildings in this city are Buland Darwaza,Jama Masjid,Tomb of Salim Chishti and Panch Mahal.

Bara Imambara

Bara Imambara complex in Lucknow is one of the most famous monuments of the city. This historical monument was built by Asaf ud Daula and among the grandest buildings of Land.

Varanasi Ghats

Ghats in Varanasi are considered as one of the holiest places in Varanasi, on the banks of the Ganges. There are 87 ghats in Varanasi, most of them are bathing and puja ceremony ghats and few are cremation sites.

Sarnath Stupa

Sarnath city is located near Varanasi and an important pilgrimage site for Buddhists. Most famous ancient buildings and structures at Sarnath are Dhamek Stupa,Ashoka Pillar and Mulagandhakuti Vihara.

Allahabad Fort

Allahabad Fort stands on the banks of the Yamuna near the confluence and one of the monument of national importance in Uttar Pradesh. The fort is one of the major tourist attractions in Allahabad along with Triveni Sangam,Patthar Girja ghar,Bade Hanumanji Temple,Khusro Bagh.

Ramnagar Fort

Ramnagar Fort in Ramnagar is located near the Ganga River in Varanasi and known for Saraswati Bhawan museum. The fort palace is an important location for outdoor shooting of incredible India.
