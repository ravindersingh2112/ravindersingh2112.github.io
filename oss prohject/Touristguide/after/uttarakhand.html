Uttarakhand

Katarmal Sun Temple

Located in Katarmal village, this revered temple sits beautifully at the height of 2,116 meters above sea level. Dedicated to Hindu God Sun, this ancient temple dates back to the 9th century and is therefore listed

Baijnath Temple

Seated in the Garhwal hills, Baijnath Temple dates back to the 12th century and is the sanctum sanctorum of Lord Shiva. This heritage temple is well-maintained and is visited by a large number of Shiva devotees from all across the country.

Bageshwar

Bageshwar is the confluence of three prominent rivers - Saryu, Gomti, and Bhagirathi. It is the famous town of Uttarakhand that is home to many significant temples from ancient times that are visited by a large number of devotees and tourists.

Ghuddauda

Located in Almora District, Ghuddauda is the only historical town in Uttarakhand that is distinctive in its own way. The town is named after a horse race that took place there during British rule.

Dwarahat

The erstwhile capital of the Katyuri Kingdom, Dwarahat is approximately 34 kilometers away from Ranikhet. Etymologically, Dwarahat means "Way to Heaven," and all the ancient temples of this town are well-known.

Badrinath

Pithoragarh Fort

It is one of the town's most famous monuments in Uttarakhand. This fort offers astonishing views of the Kali Kumaon. It is believed that Gorkhas built this fort after the town was invaded in 1789. This ravaged fort has become one of the principal forts since colonial times.

Chaukhutia

Renowned as Rangilo Gewar, Chaukhutia is a sleepy town in the Kumaon area. Growing to fame during the Katyuri rule, Chaukhutia is a palpable place to glimpse the affluent yore of Uttarakhand. The town preserves the remains of the Katyuri dynasty in the form of fort and temples.

Banasur ka Kila

Located 7km from Lohaghat in Champawat district, Banasur ka Kila is one of the best heritage places in Uttarakhand that is a reflection of its rich past. Built in the mediaeval era, this majestic fort is dedicated to the thousand-armed son of king Bali, Banasur.
